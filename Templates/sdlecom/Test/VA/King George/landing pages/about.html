Frazier-Mason Some Gave All Memorial Foundation
Director/Treasurer

The Some Gave All Foundation was established in 2007 in memory of Army Sgt. Nicholas Conan Mason of King George and Marine Sgt. Joshua James Frazier of Spotsylvania. Both men were killed in the line of duty in Iraq. The foundation honors these men and all others who have been wounded or killed in service to their country. The mission of the Some Gave All Foundation is:

  • To ensure that those who have made the ultimate sacrifice and those who have been wounded during the Global War on Terror are not forgotten.
  • To ensure these great Americans will be honored during the annual Some Gave All Motorcycle Rally, not only by their families and friends, but also a thankful nation.
  • To ensure Americaâ€™s younger generation will know of their sacrifices and how these heroes lived their lives prior to making the ultimate sacrifice.
  • To assist veterans wounded in Operation Iraqi Freedom and Operation Enduring Freedom.

Since it's estanlishment in 2007, the Foundation has awarded grants of approx. $100,000 to individuals and organizations in support of disabled veterans. The Foundation is a Bronze Sponsor of the 32nd Annual National Veterans Wheelchair Games and has awarded grants to cover the transportation cost of the McGuire Wheelchair Team for the previous three years.
