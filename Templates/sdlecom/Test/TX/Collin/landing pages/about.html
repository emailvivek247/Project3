Meet the Official

Stacey Kemp

Collin County Clerk

Stacey Kemp was elected County Clerk in November of 2006 and took her oath of office on January 1, 2007. Ms. Kemp has worked in the County Clerk's office since April of 1987 and served as Chief Deputy from 1997 to 2005.

Since taking office Ms. Kemp has been a leader in bringing innovation and technology to the office of the County Clerk. She continues to work with State Representatives' in amending legislation including HB3601, HB3602 and HB2108 allowing more efficient processes to be implemented.

Ms. Kemp's efforts have resulted in giving counties statewide the ability to post public and meeting notices electronically as well as the ability to register livestock marks and brands online. She also implemented a Property Fraud Alert program to protect citizens from fraudulent property recordings and automated the process of recording and annotating Commissioners' Court meeting minutes. Ms. Kemp has been recognized with numerous awards including the State of Texas Vital Statistic 5 Star Award, NACRC Best Practices Award, NACO Achievement Award, GCAT Collector of the Year Award and the MCCI Experience Excellence Award.

Ms. Kemp actively serves on the Collin County Bail Bond Board; the Legislative Committee for the County and District Clerk's Association and serves as Chair for the County Clerk Policy Manual Committee. She previously served on the Texas Judicial Committee on Information Technology; the National Association of County Recorders, Election Officials and Clerks (NACRC) Membership Drive Board; Co-Chair of the North Central Texas Chapter of Property Records Education Partners (PREP) and has served as Director of Community Service and Past President of the McKinney Sunrise Rotary.
